# Business Model

## Value Proposition

### For Schools
- Understand each student's learning style without testing or surveys
- Identify students who are disengaged vs thriving
- Match teaching methods to individual student profiles
- Extracurricular engagement data (games students already play)

### For Students
- Games they already play contribute to their education
- Personalized learning path matched to how they think
- No extra homework — insight comes from natural play behavior

### For Parents
- Visibility into child's learning style and engagement
- Data-driven explanation of how their child learns best

## Pricing Model

### SaaS — School Subscription
- Billed per student per month
- School admin manages roster and access
- Minimum 30 students per school (viability threshold)

| Tier | Price | Features |
|---|---|---|
| Starter | $8/student/month | API-based tracking (LoL, Valorant, CS2), edu games, basic profile, dashboard |
| Growth | $12/student/month | + Deep Behavior Mode (screen recording), advanced reports, LMS integration |
| Enterprise | Custom | + Custom edu games, dedicated support, white-label |

### Add-ons
- Deep Behavior Mode: +$3–5/student/month (screen recording + video AI)
- Parent portal access: +$1/student/month
- API access for school's own LMS: +$2/student/month

## Sales Motion

### Target Customer
- Private schools and international schools (less bureaucracy than public)
- After-school programs and tutoring centers
- Esports programs at schools (natural fit)
- EdTech coordinators and progressive principals

### Go-to-Market
1. Direct outreach to esports coaches and after-school coordinators
2. Free pilot for 1 school (3 months, 30 students) — gather testimonials
3. School referral program (3 months free for each referral)
4. Content marketing — blog posts on gaming and learning styles
5. Partnership with game publishers (Riot Games education programs)

## Compliance Requirements

### US Schools (FERPA)
- Student education records are protected
- School controls what data is shared and with whom
- Parental consent required for students under 18 for new data collection

### COPPA (under 13)
- Parental consent required before any data collection
- Consent flow must be built into onboarding

### Data Handling
- No raw video stored long-term (processed and purged)
- Student data never sold or used for ad targeting
- Schools own their student data
- Data deletion on school contract termination

## Key Risks

1. **Parental consent friction** — required for minors, may slow adoption
2. **Riot API production key** — takes 2–4 weeks, must apply early
3. **Screen recording trust** — parents may object to recording their child's screen
4. **Profile accuracy** — early model may generate poor profiles before enough data
5. **School procurement cycles** — long sales cycle, budget tied to school year
